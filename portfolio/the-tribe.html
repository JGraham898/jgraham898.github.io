The Tribe

The Company

THE TRIBE is a brand new platform designed to help founders, program organisors, investors and mentors involved in start-up accelerator programs.

1. The Problem

The goal is to offer a founder-first approach, which necessitates excellent user experience. However, as the website was set up without any initial UX research, there are some areas of confusion which need to be addressed.

2. The Solution

We set out to redesign some of the community functionality features of the website and app to ensure that users can network, upload information and find the info they are looking for in a seamless, intuitive way that is also aesthetically pleasing.

3. My Role

I led the entire project and worked on user interviews, testing and creating wireframes. I worked closely with Maxx, the founder of TRIBE.

The Process

My process for this project was based on the Design thinking process: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype and Test

Understanding the problems

The Tribe initally had an MVP release that had been implemented by the engineers without any UX design input. My first step was to conduct usability testing/research interviews with 5 primary users (startup founders) to identify any pain points they were experiencing with the MVP release.

My research involved:

  • Empathising with user needs and goals
  • Discovering pain points in current user journeys
  • Determining user satisfaction with their experience

During the interviews, I observed them interacting with the app/website and narrating their thoughts as they went through different user journeys:

  • Editing their personal profile
  • Editing their venture page and pitch deck
  • Finding and contacting other founders
  • Finding other ventures by name and industry

After the interview, I asked each person to complete a System Usability Questionnaire to give me an idea of current user satisfaction.

Framing the Design Challenge

After the interviews, I carried out affinity mapping to help me identify users' key pain points.

I then created a list of "How Might We?" questions to clarify what I needed to achieve. Before going ahead with ideation, I consulted with the company founder to identify any business matters which might affect my ability to resolve these problems.

Wireframing the Solutions

Based on the problems I identified, I mocked up some basic wireframes to gather feedback from users and the Tribe engineers. This involved rethinking information heirarchies and layouts, designing a new filtering system and providing more visual cues and information to help users achieve their goals.

Iterative Testing

After creating my initial basic wireframes, I conducted more user testing to gather feedback on the initial design ideas. The feedback was overall more positive than for the MVP, but I was able to identify some areas where I could hone the design even further.
